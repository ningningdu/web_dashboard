Latitude vs. Max Temperature
The earth's temperature peaks around equator(between latitude -20 and 0) and decreases roughly linearly as the absolute value of lantitude increases in both directions, north and south. The rate of declining is slower in southern hemisphere. This makes sense because of the obliquity of the earth and the angle of the sunlight hitting earth is more normal on the southern hemisphere. The temperature is higher when the sunlight hit the surface at a more normal angle. In another word, it is because the southern hemisphere is right now in summer.
